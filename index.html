THE PROBLEM

Centuries of inheritance have splintered hill farms into pieces too small to farm efficiently.

Chakbandi reorganizes scattered plots into contiguous, workable holdings — but doing it by hand, on Himalayan terrain, has historically taken the better part of a decade per village.

चक (Chak)
noun — land block
A defined, contiguous unit of agricultural land — the target shape every fragmented holding is consolidated into.
बंदी (Bandi)
noun — to bind / consolidate
The act of reorganizing and binding scattered parcels into those single, larger blocks.
2026 Policy Target
275
Villages across 11 hill districts, phased over 5 years, under Uttarakhand's Voluntary Chakbandi Incentive Policy.
Roughly 5 villages per district per year — a pace built for iterative, tech-assisted rollout.
WHY IT'S HARD IN THE HILLS
01

Fragmented plots

Generations of inheritance split land into tiny, scattered holdings — often dozens per family.

02

Survey inaccuracy

Old cadastral maps rarely match ground reality once you account for slope and terracing.

03

Slow process

Manual chakbandi has historically taken eight or more years to complete per village.

04

Boundary disputes

Imprecise demarcation between neighboring plots fuels ongoing legal conflict.

05

Out-migration

Unviable, scattered farms push the working-age population toward cities.

06

Low productivity

Mechanization and irrigation planning are nearly impossible on disconnected terraces.

THE FRAMEWORK

Three layers, one pipeline.

Drones capture the ground truth. GIS turns it into structured, correctable records. AI solves the redistribution puzzle fairly — at a pace manual chakbandi never could.

GIS-Based Land Record Modernization

Drone imagery is overlaid on existing khasra/khatauni records, automatically flagging every boundary mismatch. Each parcel gets a digital ULPIN ID, and multi-layer scoring ensures fair, value-equivalent land swaps.

AI-Powered Optimization & Planning

A linear-programming model solves the redistribution puzzle across hundreds of parcels in seconds — balancing land-value equity, contiguity, and farmer preference, while flagging swaps likely to trigger disputes.

ROLLOUT PLAN

Five years, four stages.

Aligned to the 2026 Voluntary Chakbandi Incentive Policy's pace of five villages per district per year — built so the AI improves with every village it touches.

YEAR 1

Pilot & Setup
